Freqently Asked Questions

What deity is worshipped here?

The temple is dedicated to Lord Ganesha in the form of Siddhivinayak, who is widely worshipped as the remover of obstacles and bestower of success.

When does the temple open?

According to local listings, the temple opens around 7:00 AM every day.

Is the temple open every day?

Yes — it is generally open Monday through Sunday, and devotees visit daily.

Is there any contact number?

A contact number listed on business directories is 08419 238380. (Please note: this may vary, so calling ahead before visiting is recommended).

What do visitors say about the temple?

Reviews describe it as a peaceful and spiritually uplifting place, ideal for devotees and even for family trips. Some also refer to it locally as Gante Ganapati.

Travel & Location Guide

By Road

Yellapur is the nearest reference point. Chandguli is accessible via local roads branching from Yellapur and villages like Magod and Belakanda.

Tip: From Hubli, take the Kumta–Sirsi–Yellapur route. Hire an auto/taxi from Yellapur.

Best Time to Visit

Morning: 7:00 AM – 10:00 AM for a peaceful darshan.

Festivals: Ganesh Chaturthi is a special occasion, attracting more devotees for special rituals.

Nearby Places

  • Magod Falls: A popular waterfall near Yellapur.
  • Belakanda: Scenic landscapes ideal for photography.
